They say show, don't tell. So I stopped telling!

Every portfolio website I opened looked the same. A grid of cards, a skills section, a contact form. Dead on arrival.
So I asked myself a different question: what if my portfolio wasn't a document but a film?

I built a piece of my life as one continuous camera shot. You scroll, and the camera flies from a 3D torus knot spinning in the studio I started at 12, through a blueprint of my school International School Lahore building itself, straight through the arch of my university gate, into a city of data at Systems Limited, through the ring of eight circles at cirkles, and lands inside my AI security research where you literally watch prompt injections get blocked by the verifier I'm building.

My face on the first page? Around 40,000 particles! sampled live from a photo. The text turns to outlines and my portrait shows through the letters.

this is just a fraction of the ideas I planned. 

Portfolios aren't dead. We just stopped directing them.
